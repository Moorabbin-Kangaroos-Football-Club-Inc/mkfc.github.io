---

## Recommendation: pick one canonical handle

Choose a single handle and use it everywhere we can. Decide first, claim second.

### Option A — `moorabbinkangas` (matches new Facebook)

- **Pros:** consistent with the recently updated Facebook handle; "Kangas" is the local nickname; distinctive.
- **Cons:** less SEO weight than the full club name; "Kangas" is less recognised outside the Bayside region.
- **Verdict:** strong if we're committing to "Kangas" as the colloquial brand voice.

### Option B — `moorabbinroos`

- **Pros:** already claimed on Instagram and Twitter — zero migration cost on those platforms; "Roos" is the most universally recognised AFL nickname; short.
- **Cons:** would mean reverting the Facebook change.
- **Verdict:** lowest-effort path. Keep this if Facebook can be moved back.

### Option C — `moorabbinkangaroos`

- **Pros:** matches the official club name; best SEO; unambiguous.
- **Cons:** long (close to character limits on some platforms); harder to fit in printed material; previous Facebook handle.
- **Verdict:** safe and professional, but longer typing experience.

### Option D — `mkfc`

- **Pros:** very short, brandable, fits anywhere.
- **Cons:** almost certainly already taken on most platforms; "MKFC" is also Mansfield Town, Maidenhead United, MK Forest, etc. — heavy collision risk; loses local discoverability.
- **Verdict:** not recommended as primary; consider as a fallback prefix (e.g. `mkfc_au`, `mkfcsfnl`).

### My recommendation

**Option B (`moorabbinroos`)** if reverting Facebook is feasible — minimum churn, broadest recognition.
**Option A (`moorabbinkangas`)** if we're committing to the new Facebook handle — but then update Instagram and Twitter to match.

Whichever we pick, the goal is **one handle, every platform.**
